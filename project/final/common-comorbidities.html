Common Comorbid Mood Disorders

  • Major Depressive Disorder

    Major depressive disorder is one of the most prevalent comorbidities in adults with ADHD. Adults with ADHD are three times more likely to develop MDD compared to the general population12. Approximately 70% of adults with ADHD will be treated for depression at some point in their lives4.

  • Anxiety Disorders

    Anxiety disorders are another frequent comorbidity, with more than half of people with ADHD experiencing an anxiety disorder such as social anxiety disorder, panic disorder, or post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). The presence of anxiety can exacerbate ADHD symptoms and vice versa, making proper diagnosis and treatment crucial.

  • Dysthymia

    Adults with ADHD are six times more likely to develop dysthymia, a persistent depressive disorder characterized by long-term (chronic) depression. This condition can significantly impact quality of life and daily functioning.

  • Bipolar Disorder

    Bipolar disorder, particularly bipolar I disorder, is another common comorbidity with ADHD. Studies have reported that roughly 5-7% of people with ADHD also have bipolar disorder. The comorbidity rates of ADHD in individuals with bipolar disorder range from 9.5% to 21.2%.

    Increased Severity:

    Patients with comorbid ADHD-BD experience: More depressive, mixed, hypomanic, and total mood episodes. Shorter periods of wellness. Higher instances of additional comorbid conditions, including anxiety and substance use disorders.
