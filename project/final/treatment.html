Common Treatment Options

Living with ADHD can be really difficult and it's important to understand you're not alone and know ways to improve your quality of life.

  • Major Depressive Disorder and Dysthymia

    Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT):

    Effective for addressing negative thought patterns and behaviors associated with depression.

    Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs):

    Commonly prescribed antidepressants that can be used alongside ADHD medications.

    Lifestyle Modifications:

    Incorporating regular physical activity, healthy eating, and sleep hygiene can support overall mental health.

    Bipolar Disorder

    Mood Stabilizers:

    Medications such as lithium or valproate are used to manage mood swings.

    Psychoeducation:

    Teaching patients about the disorder to help them recognize symptoms early and manage episodes effectively.

    Staged Treatment Approach:

    Initiating treatment for bipolar disorder first before addressing ADHD symptoms can prevent exacerbation of mood episodes due to stimulant medications.

    Anxiety Disorders

    Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT):

    Particularly effective for anxiety, helping individuals challenge and change unhelpful thoughts and behaviors.

    SSRIs or SNRIs:

    Antidepressants that can alleviate anxiety symptoms while being compatible with ADHD treatments.

    Mindfulness-Based Therapies:

    Techniques such as mindfulness meditation can reduce anxiety symptoms and improve emotional regulation.
